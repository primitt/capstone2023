THE INDEPENDANT INDIA

Mahatma Gandhi's ambition leading India to independence.
By: Priansh Mittra, August 15, 1947

Today, it is a momentous occasion in the Durbar Hall in New Delhi, listening to Jawaharlal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India, proclaim, "I am proud to announce that India is now a free country. We have fought for this day for many years, and now, we have finally achieved it. We have finally achieved our freedom."
This story of triumph for India's independence cannot be written without the unwavering commitment and ambition of Mahatma Gandhi, who led the powerful non-violent movement for end of the Britain's imperialistic rule. Today is a day we can reflect on the power of noble ambitions leading to the a positive change in the lives of millions of people of the nation while acknowledging the devastating results of World Wars due to unchecked ambitions of the world leaders, resulting in massive destruction.
The ambitions of the world leaders during the past many decades have fueled by imperialistic desires economic gains and thirst of power. As shown in great literary works like All Is Quiet On the Western Front shows the destructive natures of senseless and horrific wars.

Mahatma Gandhi's life serves as an example of the tranformatave power of ambition and it serves as a reminder that nobel ambitions can lead to positive changes and inspire generations to come. As the nations of the world emerges from the devastations of the Great War, Mahatma Gandhi's ambition of peaceful revolution is an inspiration for all of us to create a compassionate and just society for all.
